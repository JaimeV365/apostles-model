🛠️ Using the Tool

The Apostles Model divides customers into four quadrants based on satisfaction and loyalty:

  • 👑 Apostles (High Satisfaction, High Loyalty): Your best customers who love your products and stick with you
  • ⭐ Loyalists (Low Satisfaction, High Loyalty): They stick with you but there's room for improvement
  • ⚠️ Defectors (High Satisfaction, Low Loyalty): They're happy but might leave soon
  • 🚨 Terrorists (Low Satisfaction, Low Loyalty): Neither satisfied nor loyal - potential negative word-of-mouth

Apostles are your goldmine customers:

  • Focus on retaining them and turning them into advocates
  • Ask for referrals and testimonials
  • Consider them for beta testing new features

Terrorists need immediate attention:

  • Reach out personally to understand their concerns
  • Address their issues quickly to prevent negative word-of-mouth
  • Consider if they're worth retaining or if it's time to part ways

Near-Apostles are customers who are close to becoming full Apostles but need a small push. Look for:

  • High satisfaction (4-5) but moderate loyalty (3-4)
  • Customers who love your product but haven't fully committed
  • Recent customers with high satisfaction scores

Enable the "Show Near-Apostles" option in the tool to highlight these customers. Focus on converting them to full Apostles through loyalty programs, exclusive offers, or personalized engagement.

📊 Understanding the Model

The Apostles Model is a customer segmentation framework that classifies customers into four groups based on two dimensions: satisfaction and loyalty. It was developed by Thomas O. Jones and W. Earl Sasser Jr. in their groundbreaking 1995 Harvard Business Review article "Why Satisfied Customers Defect."

Segmentor.app is built on this proven framework—it's the Apostles Model revamped for modern times with updated terminology and enhanced analytics.

The original model identified four customer types:

  • Apostles (now called Advocates): High satisfaction + High loyalty
  • Mercenaries: High satisfaction + Low loyalty
  • Hostages: Low satisfaction + High loyalty
  • Terrorists (now called Trolls): Low satisfaction + Low loyalty

Why it matters: The model revealed that satisfaction alone doesn't predict loyalty. A customer can be completely satisfied yet still defect to competitors (Mercenaries), while unhappy customers might stay due to switching costs (Hostages).

Segmentor.app brings this powerful framework into the 21st century with modern terminology, interactive visualization, and advanced analytics.

Academic citation: Jones, T. O., & Sasser, W. E., Jr. (1995). Why Satisfied Customers Defect. Harvard Business Review, 73(6), 88-99.

Net Promoter Score (NPS) measures one dimension; the Apostles Model measures two. This gives you a much richer understanding of your customers.

NPS tells you:

  • How likely customers are to recommend you (loyalty)
  • One single number: -100 to +100

The Apostles Model (Segmentor.app) tells you:

  • How satisfied customers are (satisfaction)
  • How loyal customers are (loyalty/advocacy)
  • Which of four distinct groups each customer belongs to
  • Specific actions needed for each segment

Real-world example:
Imagine two customers with NPS score of 8 (Promoters):

  • Customer A: Satisfied but only loyal because no alternatives exist (Hostage)
  • Customer B: Genuinely loves your product and recommends it actively (Advocate)

NPS treats them the same. The Apostles Model reveals the critical difference.

Use both together: NPS is great for tracking trends over time. The Apostles Model is better for understanding why customers behave the way they do and what actions to take.

An Advocate (Apostle in the original terminology) is a customer with both high satisfaction AND high loyalty—your best customers who genuinely love what you do.

Characteristics of Advocates:

  • ✅ Highly satisfied with your product/service
  • ✅ Actively recommend you to others
  • ✅ Resist competitive offers
  • ✅ Forgive occasional mistakes
  • ✅ Provide valuable feedback
  • ✅ Drive most of your organic growth

In Segmentor.app:
Advocates appear in the top-right corner of the visualization grid. They score high on both axes: typically 8-10 on satisfaction and 9-10 on loyalty (on a 1-10 scale).

The enhanced model also identifies Near-Advocates—customers who are close to Advocate status and represent your best opportunity for conversion with targeted improvements.

Why they matter: Research shows that Advocates have significantly higher lifetime value, lower acquisition costs, and generate positive word-of-mouth that brings new customers at zero marketing cost.

Your mission: Protect your Advocates (keep doing what makes them happy) and create more Advocates by moving Near-Advocates up and to the right.

We use modern, inclusive language while maintaining the framework's integrity. The original 1995 model used terms that made sense then but feel outdated today.

Original → Modern terminology:

  • Apostles → Advocates: More secular and professional
  • Terrorists → Trolls: Removes violent connotations while keeping the meaning (destructive critics)
  • Mercenaries & Hostages: Unchanged (still relevant metaphors)

When we reference the original research:
We use the original terminology (Apostles, Terrorists) to properly credit Jones and Sasser's work. The academic framework remains identical—only the labels have been modernized.

Segmentor.app is "the Apostles Model on steroids"—we've taken the proven framework and enhanced it with:

  • Interactive real-time visualization
  • Advanced proximity analysis
  • Historical tracking capabilities
  • Modern data science techniques

Think of it as a respectful revision made from admiration, bringing a timeless framework into modern business practice.
