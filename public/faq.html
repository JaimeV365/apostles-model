What is the Apostles Model?

The Apostles Model is a customer segmentation framework that classifies customers into four groups based on two dimensions: satisfaction and loyalty. It was developed by Thomas O. Jones and W. Earl Sasser Jr. in their groundbreaking 1995 Harvard Business Review article "Why Satisfied Customers Defect."

The original model identified four customer types:

  • Apostles (now called *Advocates*): High satisfaction + High loyalty
  • Mercenaries: High satisfaction + Low loyalty
  • Hostages: Low satisfaction + High loyalty
  • Terrorists (now called *Trolls*): Low satisfaction + Low loyalty

How is this different from NPS?

Net Promoter Score (NPS) measures one dimension; the Apostles Model measures two. This gives you a much richer understanding of your customers.

NPS tells you: How likely customers are to recommend you (loyalty) - One single number: -100 to +100

The Apostles Model (Segmentor.app) tells you:

  • How satisfied customers are (satisfaction)
  • How loyal customers are (loyalty/advocacy)
  • Which of four distinct groups each customer belongs to
  • Specific actions needed for each segment
